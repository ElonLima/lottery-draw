Method 1: Multiplicative Congruential

Multiplicative Congruential Method is a type of linear congruential generator for generating pseudorandom numbers in a specific range.

  • Choose the seed value ( X0 ), modulus parameter ( m ), and multiplier term ( a ).
  • Initialize the required amount of random numbers to generate (say, an integer variable noOfRandomNums).
  • Define a storage to keep the generated random numbers (here, vector is considered) of size noOfRandomNums.
  • Initialize the 0th index of the vector with the seed value.
  • For rest of the indexes follow the Multiplicative Congruential Method to generate the random numbers.
  • Finally, return the random numbers.

    • The efficiency of these generators is higher when m is raised to the power of two, so the operation mod becomes trivial.
